The Wave That Changed Everything

Story Narration

I was 12 years old when the ocean pulled back and left the shore dry. Curious villagers walked out to collect flopping fish, but my grandmother screamed for us to run. The ground shook as we climbed the hill, and moments later a wall of water swallowed the coast. Houses vanished. Boats smashed into trees. I clung to my brother's hand and promised we would survive.

Days later, the sea was silent. We slept in a school, sharing rice and stories with other families who had nothing left. Volunteers came with clean water and warm blankets, and slowly the fear turned into determination. We rebuilt our home with bamboo and hope, learning evacuation routes and the sound of sirens. The ocean that once took everything now reminds us to prepare and to care for one another.
