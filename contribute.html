The Wave That Changed Everything

Anonymous Survivor • Banda Aceh
Tsunami Family Survival

"I was 12 years old when the water came. The sound still haunts me - like a thousand trains coming at once. My father recognized it immediately as the 'Smong' our elders had warned about. He grabbed me and my siblings and we ran to the hills behind our house. We lost everything that day - our home, our photos, our school books. But what I remember most is how neighbors who had been fighting for years suddenly became family again. In the refugee camp, former enemies shared what little food they had. That's the lesson I carry with me - disaster doesn't care about politics or religion. When the wave comes, we're all just human beings trying to survive."

Educational Insight

This story illustrates the importance of both traditional knowledge (Smong) and community solidarity in disaster response. It also shows how shared trauma can become a foundation for reconciliation.
